# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A single hand-built ceramic vessel flute photographed as a finished physical object: a rounded, palm-sized hollow ocarina-style body with a smooth bulbous resonating cavity, no open tube — the silhouette reads as a closed clay vessel rather than a pipe. The signature feature is the voicing assembly: a flattened mouthpiece spout with a rectangular windway leading to a crisp cut voicing window and a clean labium edge angled into the cavity, the detail that makes a Helmholtz vessel flute recognizable. A scattering of small round finger holes dots the upper face, suggesting an undersize-then-enlarged tuning workflow without any callouts. Earthenware material and finish implied by the design: matte unglazed terracotta clay with subtle hand-tool burnishing and faint fingerprint texture, gently fired warm reddish-brown surface. Realistic scale cues from the palm-fitting body and gentle wall thickness. Clean neutral light-gray studio background, soft directional studio light raking across the voicing window, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same ceramic vessel flute resting on a potter's worn wooden workbench in a ceramics lab, beside a small graduated water-fill measuring beaker and a set of clay shaping tools, a few unfired test tiles drying nearby. Warm natural window light from the side, shallow depth of field with the voicing window in focus and the background tools softly blurred, environmental storytelling of a volume-and-voicing validation bench. Concept visualization only, photorealistic, no text labels.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the voicing assembly on the ceramic vessel flute: the flattened mouthpiece spout, the narrow rectangular windway channel, the sharp-cut voicing window, and the angled labium edge where the air sheet splits. Show the matte clay texture, hand-burnished surface, faint tool marks, and the crisp ceramic edge of the labium. Raking low-angle light to emphasize the cut geometry and surface grain, very high detail, shallow depth of field, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no open-ended tube/recorder body or impossible windway geometry; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
